codetiger · one system

A deep-navy cinematic console

The whole codetiger family shares one identity, derived from the single-page résumé: a near-black navy canvas, cyan-tinted hairlines instead of grey borders, a blue-white text ladder, and a cyan / gold / mint accent trio that glows rather than casts shadow. Depth comes from 1px cyan lines, a notch-lighter surface, and soft glow — not from heavy drop shadows.

Cyan is the contract

Cyan = link, hover, focus, current, and the one primary action. Gold = dates & external links. Mint = pills & success.

One identity, all rooms

Landing, blog, résumé, Avarta and Vishwakarma all wear the same navy/cyan skin — no per-app hue divergence.

Glow, then shadow

Cyan glow on the avatar & headings. Hard shadows are reserved for floating chrome — dropdowns, HUD panels, modals.

Try the toggles. Backdrop (top-right) flips the signature radial-glow + 44px grid backdrop on/off — it ships as the opt-in .brand-backdrop utility. Theme switches to the light/ink mode used for the résumé PDF.
Built on modern, Baseline CSS — no build step. Colours are OKLCH (perceptual, P3-ready); theming is one light-dark() per token; borders and tints derive from the accent seed via relative-colour syntax; components live in @layer (so your own CSS overrides them with no specificity war). The guide also adapts to OS accessibility settings — set forced-colors (high contrast), reduced transparency, increased contrast or reduced motion in your OS (or DevTools → Rendering) to watch it respond.
